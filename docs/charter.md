## The Philosophy

Art should make you care. Art should make you think. A game that holds your
imagination long enough for you to challenge yourself is a game that helps you
grow. This fighting game is built as art with that intent — it earns your
curiosity, then rewards it.

## North Star

Move the player from *"I need to be good enough to play this"* to *"I'm having
fun exploring the play space."*

The barrier we tear down is **comprehension, not difficulty**. The game is
always *legible* — you can find out what happened and why, every time — even
when the thing you're trying to understand is genuinely hard. Transparency is
the promise; instant ease is not. Mastery is never necessarily easy.

Reaching true, holistic understanding of the game should be deeply satisfying
no matter how short or long the path there is. The work of understanding is
itself part of the reward.

## For Veterans

Genre knowledge pays off immediately — a veteran reads this game fast and feels
the system open up under them. That clarity of perception lets them teach
others — an aspiration we enable through the game's legibility and clarity, not
a feature we claim to have built outright.

This is not Fantasy Strike or Footsies; nothing is dumbed down. Hard,
character-specific combos exist, and landing one should feel incredible — like
touching the ceiling of everything a character can do. The ceiling is real and
high. Only the path to *understanding* it is made gentle.
